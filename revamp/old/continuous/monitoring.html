Monitoring and measuring task success: Continuous improvement of web content

On this page

  • Measuring task performance
  • Monitoring issues
  • Continuous improvement

By following the digital standards and making evidence-based decisions, you’ve become good at building and launching web content and services. Now you need to find ways to keep them performing well to avoid high-effort optimization projects in the future.

Measuring task performance

For continuous improvement to work well, you’ll need to establish performance measurement metrics (key performance indicators KPIs) and identify data sources that will help you evaluate how your content or service is performing.

For Canada.ca, our main goal is to make sure the web channel is useful for Canadians.

We can use data to make sure that:

  • people can complete their task
  • content is easy to find and understand
  • information is up to date and accurate

The primary indicator of task success will come from your task success survey. This quantitative measure will help identify trends with task performance and ease of use. User submitted feedback can help pinpoint what parts of the service may have issues to explore in more detail.

Monitoring issues

Performance monitoring goes beyond sharing dashboards of web analytics. Monitoring your KPIs for issues can uncover potential problems with your content or service. By identifying potential problems early on, you can initiate research and testing plans to get your task back on track.

Combining data from multiple sources will give you a richer picture of how your content is performing.

Additional data sources include:

  • user feedback
  • call centre data (calls, emails)
  • social media monitoring
  • web analytics
  • interviews (users, front-line staff)
  • usability tests with talk-aloud protocol
  • others (financial statements, public opinion research, etc.)

It’s best to combine metrics with usability testing to validate proposed improvements.

Continuous improvement

Use the data you’ve been monitoring (such as user feedback, analytics, and user research) to find ways to improve your content.

Over time, programs and policies will change and your content will need to change with it. With each change comes a new opportunity to research, prototype, and test out solutions before publishing.

You should repeat the research, prototype, and testing processes for smaller pieces of work.
