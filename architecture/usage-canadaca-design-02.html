Who has to use the Canada.ca design system: Content and Information Architecture Specification

Federal public administration listed in Schedules I, I.1 and II of the Financial Administration Act must apply Canada.ca design requirements for all their public-facing websites or digital services.

Directives and Acts

The Directive on the management of Communications and the Financial Administration Act state what government organizations must follow the Content and Information Architecture Specification.

Directive on the Management of Communications

The Canada.ca Content and Information Architecture Specification is referenced in "Appendix D: Mandatory Procedures for Social Media and Web Communications" of the Directive on the Management of Communications. It is the directive that guides the publication of The government of Canada's digital assets.

  • Directive on the Management of Communications

Financial Administration Act

Directive on the Management of Communications' requirements apply to departments and other portions of the federal public administration listed in the Financial Administration Act:

  • Financial Administration Act - Schedule I
  • Financial Administration Act - Schedule I.1
  • Financial Administration Act - Schedule II

Institutions required to use the Canada.ca design

Consult the Institutions list to determine which organizations must apply Canada.ca design requirements. This includes all their public-facing websites or digital services, regardless of the technology, domain name (both canada.ca and gc.ca) or publishing platform through which these are delivered.

  • Institutions list for Canada.ca

Out-of-scope situations

The Canada.ca global header and footer design may not be applied or replicated in the following situations:

  • internal-facing websites or digital services
  • Government of Canada institutions that are not subject to the Directive on the Management of Communications
  • enterprises and organizations that are not part of the Government of Canada
